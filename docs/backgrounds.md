# Backgrounds

Auto-generated by `scripts/backgrounds/write-doc.ts` — do not edit by hand. Regenerate after any wallpaper change.

## How selection works

The app picks a wallpaper based on two axes:

- **Time segment** — dawn / morning / afternoon / goldenHour / evening / night, derived from the local hour (`getTimeSegment` in `@brett/business`).
- **Busyness tier** — light / moderate / packed, derived from today's meeting + task count vs. the user's 14-day baseline (`getBusynessTier`).

Within a slot, images rotate every 10 minutes with shuffle-without-replacement (`selectImage`).

Each wallpaper has two physical files: a **landscape** (2560×1440, desktop) and a **portrait** (1290×2796, iOS) crop.

On iOS the middle 50–65% band of the landscape is sampled to a solid **wash color** that paints the bed for non-Today pages (`WashColorSampler.swift`). The wash hex is included below per image — useful when you're designing something that has to read against the wallpaper bed.

## Testing patterns

When designing UI that sits on a wallpaper (glass cards, briefing prose, nav, hero text), test against the extremes from the wash column above:

- The **lightest** washes (`#C0+`) stress dark-on-light readability — watch for white text disappearing, glass cards getting overpowered.
- The **darkest** washes (`#3-`) stress light-on-dark — watch for glass tinting getting muddy, text-shadow shifting.
- The **most saturated** washes (deep amber / cobalt / teal) stress neutral chrome — does the gold accent fight the wallpaper?
- The **portrait** crop is always center-vertical on iOS, so if a landscape's interest is in the left or right thirds, the portrait will look different from the desktop — check both columns.

Open `scripts/backgrounds/gallery.html` (rebuild with `pnpm tsx scripts/backgrounds/build-gallery.ts` and serve via `pnpm preview` config) for an at-a-glance side-by-side with the real Today UI overlaid.
