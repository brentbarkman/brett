Briefing spec
What to watch for: at V3 the scrim is doing real work but still presents as a soft atmospheric drift. By V4 it starts to look like a header bar rather than an ambient layer — the wallpaper character begins to hide under it. The honest sweet spot is usually somewhere between V2 and V3, and the right answer depends on whether you want the scrim to disappear on dark wallpapers or always be present.

If you pick V3: on darker wallpapers (Monument Valley, evening/packed) the scrim becomes invisible anyway because the underlying pixels are already near-black — so V3-on-bright behaves like V1-on-dark in practice. That argues for "always-on at V3" being safe.

If you pick V2: we may want adaptive opacity — light wallpapers boost to ~V3, dark ones stay at V1/V2. Slightly more code but the system feels smarter.
